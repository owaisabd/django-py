What is Mental Health?

Mental health includes our emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It affects how we think, feel, and act, and helps determine how we handle stress, relate to others, and make choices. Mental health is important at every stage of life, from childhood and adolescence through adulthood. Over the course of your life, if you experience mental health problems, your thinking, mood, and behavior could be affected.

Mental Health is Vital: Mental health is as crucial as physical health. It encompasses emotional, psychological, and social well-being and affects how we think, feel, and act.

Normalizing Discussions: Encourage open conversations about mental health to break the stigma. Normalizing discussions can help people feel more comfortable seeking help.

Signs and Symptoms: Awareness of common signs and symptoms of mental health issues such as anxiety, depression, mood swings, changes in behavior, and withdrawal from activities.

Reducing Stress: Educate about managing stress as excessive stress can negatively impact mental health. Techniques like relaxation exercises, time management, and prioritization can help.
